## Setup and ownership notes

Before buying, think about floor type, thresholds, rug edges, pet hair, furniture spacing, and where the charging area will live. The robot needs a realistic path through the home, and the dock needs enough clearance to remain accessible. App mapping and room naming are worth doing carefully because they can make scheduled cleaning and zone cleaning more useful. Revisit the map after furniture moves or a change in room layout rather than assuming the first setup will remain ideal forever.

For the first few cycles, supervise the routine enough to notice cables, curtains, lightweight mats, or locations where the robot cannot return cleanly to the dock. This is ordinary setup diligence, not evidence of a defect. It also gives the household a chance to decide whether whole-home schedules, room schedules, or smaller zones better match traffic patterns. Replacement consumables, bag availability, and support terms are worth confirming from the current seller before purchase.
